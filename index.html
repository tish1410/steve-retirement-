The Early Days: Finding the Calling

Apprenticeship & First Jobs

So how did it all begin?

Well, our man Steve didn’t come out of the womb selling sofas— No, it all started when his dad—a tailor—had a friend in the furniture trade. Steve got to know him and thought, “Yeah, I can do this.” So he trained up as an upholsterer, which is basically furniture whispering.

From there, Steve landed his first job at Beales of Poole—a classy place where the floor sold both furniture and electricals. Steve was on the furniture side, but was dangerously good at selling TVs too. That didn’t sit well with the electrical guys. Turns out, they didn’t appreciate him flogging tellies and the sofas to watch them from.

London is Calling

Moving to the Big City & TANDY Success

Then love struck—Steve met his wife, moved to London, and started working for TANDY in Bexleyheath. He did so well there that they sent him to fix Croydon, and what did he do? Only went and doubled the store’s volume. How? By introducing a bold, new retail concept: “Selling stuff to customers.” Revolutionary.

While working miracles in Croydon, Steve was headhunted by DFS—yes, actual headhunted, back when it didn’t just mean a dodgy message on LinkedIn. He impressed the recruiter so much, he got a bottle of Châteauneuf-du-Pape as a thank you.

The DFS Era Begins

Headhunted & The Sidcup Start

Steve started at Sidcup, and the moment he got his first DFS pay packet, he ran to show his old boss—“Look! I’ve doubled my money!”  

After five years in Sidcup, he was asked to cover Croydon.  — he soon realised... he was making more money! Why? Well, partly because Ali Mehmet and Roy Webb were still in Sidcup and selling like it was an Olympic sport. Ali even numbered his order pad from 1 to 10 every day—just to tick off how many sales he'd make. Steve thought, “Alright, let’s give that a go.” And sure enough, it worked!

The OKR Chapter

Record-Breaking

Then came 2011 and the big one: OKR was opening. But you couldn’t just stroll in. No, Steve had to take a test. It was like DFS Hogwarts. Luckily, Christian Galloway gave the nod, and Steve walked in... and walked out top book on opening day.  The store booked a casual £204K. Light work for Steve and his colleagues, but the best opening day we know of.

He stayed at OKR for 9 years before returning to his roots in Sidcup—and according to him, it’s the nicest team he’s ever worked with. People want to support and help each other. Not like the “aggressive sharks” from the old days. And we believe him—because back in Croydon, one salesman literally punched him in the ribs just for standing at the front of the store. Another time, two blokes started a fight over who got to book a deal... and the manager broke it up, then booked the deal himself. Power move.

Memorable Moments

Personal & Professional Milestones

A memorable year? Hard to choose, but probably 2015. His eldest daughter got married, he sold his parents’ house, paid off his mortgage, and had the kind of peace most of us only find in a DFS recliner.

There were other high points too: when Lord Kirkham bought back all the shares, Steve got enough for a new roof on his house. Stylish and leak-free.

He got into the Millionaires Club, [what happens in Paris. stays in Paris] He won CIN, YAY! That got your daughter her wedding dress! And—fittingly—wrapped up his career by winning the Retail Values Award in his final month. Now that’s a mic drop.

Thank You from everyone!

A Final Word

Steve, we salute you. You’ve weathered sales battles, bruised ribs, booming weekends, weird customer stories, and some truly competitive teammates—and you’ve done it all with humour, heart, and have remained genuine throughout. Thank you for everything. And enjoy your well-deserved retirement—you’ve earned every drop of it (including that Châteauneuf-du-Pape).
